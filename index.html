Research

Optimal Execution under Changing Cost Regimes

2025 – present

Reinforcement learning for optimal trade execution, advised by Dr. Alessio Brini (Duke). Instead of training across markets and hoping for generalization, the agent estimates its current cost environment online, rolling spread, available depth, recent volatility, and realized slippage, and conditions execution on that estimate: trading aggressively when liquidity is cheap, waiting or posting passively when it is costly, and accelerating near the deadline. The work measures robustness to controlled out-of-distribution shifts in liquidity-cost and timing-risk regimes, benchmarking cost-conditioned RL against TWAP, single-regime RL, and plain multi-regime RL by implementation shortfall (bps) in the ABIDES-Gym market simulator.

Beyond

Away from the terminal, I ski and surf whenever the season allows, and I spend much of my free time on photography. To me, photography is not simply about preserving a beautiful view or freezing a moment in time. Every photograph reflects a choice: what I noticed, what I left outside the frame, and why that particular scene made me pause. The moment I press the shutter often captures my own thoughts and emotions as much as it captures the subject in front of me. Sometimes a photo becomes meaningful only much later, when I look back and realize how closely it was tied to a certain period of my life. I enjoy the quiet attention that photography requires, especially the feeling of finding something worth remembering in an ordinary place. Through photographs, I can revisit not only what I saw, but also how I felt and who I was at that moment.
